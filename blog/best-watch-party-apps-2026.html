Watch Party Apps in 2026: Complete Comparison Guide

Roan de GraafJanuary 10, 2026Updated February 3, 202615 min read

Looking for a watch party app to sync video with friends? Whether you're searching for a Watch2Gether alternative or Teleparty alternative, choosing the right platform can make a difference in your remote viewing experience. This guide compares the major options to help you decide.

Last verified: February 2026. Pricing and features may change.

What Makes a Great Watch Party App?

Before diving into individual apps, here's what to consider when choosing a watch party platform:

  • Sync quality: How well does playback stay synchronized across all viewers?
  • Platform support: Does it work with the video sources you actually use (YouTube, Netflix, Twitch, etc.)?
  • Ease of access: What setup is required—downloads, extensions, or account creation?
  • Communication options: Built-in chat, voice chat, or video chat?
  • Privacy controls: Private rooms and control over who can join
  • Cost: Free tier availability and premium pricing
  • Mobile support: Works on phones and tablets, not just desktop

Watch Party Apps Compared

1. Watch4Gether

Watch4Gether is a browser-based watch party platform focused on simplicity. No account creation required, and it supports YouTube, Twitch, Kick, and other HTML5 video sites natively.

Key Features:

  • No signup required—share a link and start watching
  • Real-time sync with Socket.IO technology
  • Private rooms by default with shareable codes
  • Dark mode interface
  • Free to use
  • Playlist management with voting support
  • Role-based permissions (host, moderator, viewer)
  • Works on mobile browsers

Best For: YouTube watch parties, Twitch streams, users who want quick setup without account creation.

  • Pricing: Free
  • Supports: YouTube, Twitch, Kick, HTML5 video sites; streaming services via browser extension

2. Watch2Gether (w2g.tv)

A long-standing option that pioneered the synchronized watching space.Watch2Gether offers public room discovery features and a wide range of supported platforms.

  • Supports: YouTube, Vimeo, Dailymotion, Twitch, SoundCloud, TikTok, Instagram, Twitter, Facebook
  • Features: Text chat, optional webcam (up to 10 users), screen sharing
  • Pricing: Free tier available; Watch2Gether PLUS at $2.90/month (billed annually at $34.80); PRO tier also available
  • Mobile: No dedicated app, but responsive website works on mobile browsers

Best For: Users who want to browse public rooms and discover content with others, or need support for social media platforms.

3. Teleparty (Formerly Netflix Party)

The go-to choice for streaming service users. Teleparty requires a browser extension and integrates with major streaming platforms.

  • Supports: 9 services free (Netflix, YouTube, Disney+, Max, etc.); 23+ with Premium
  • Features: Built-in chat; Premium adds video chat, custom reactions
  • Pricing: Free tier available; Premium $3.99-$6.59/month or $47.88/year
  • Mobile: Android app available (beta); iOS requires Premium subscription
  • Note: Extension required for desktop

Best For: Groups who want to watch Netflix, Disney+, or other streaming services together (everyone needs their own subscription).

4. OpenTogetherTube

An open-source alternative with transparent development. Supports YouTube, Vimeo, Dailymotion, direct .mp4 links, and PeerTube.

  • Features: Voting system, room permissions, dark theme, no registration required
  • Pricing: 100% free and open source
  • Self-hosting: Can be self-hosted for full control

Best For: Privacy-conscious users, open-source advocates, and those who want to self-host.

5. Scener

A theater-style experience with built-in video and audio chat. Scener partners with streaming services and hosts community watch parties.

  • Supports: Netflix, Disney+, Prime Video, HBO Max, Hotstar, Shudder, YouTube
  • Features: Up to 10 people with video/audio chat; unlimited text-only guests in theaters
  • Pricing: Free to use with Premium tier available (pricing varies)
  • Mobile: iOS app available; no Android app
  • Note: Chrome extension required for desktop

Best For: Users who want video chat built in and a social theater experience.

6. Rave

Mobile-first app with voice chat and social discovery features. Rave has native apps for multiple platforms.

  • Supports: YouTube, Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+, HBO Max, Google Drive, Vimeo
  • Features: Voice chat built-in, text chat
  • Pricing: Free with optional Premium for ad-free experience
  • Platforms: iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, Chromebook, AndroidTV

Best For: Mobile-first users who want native apps and built-in voice chat.

7. Hyperbeam

A shared virtual browser environment where everyone controls the same screen. Works with any website since you're sharing a browser session.

  • Supports: Any website (shared virtual browser)
  • Features: Collaborative control, no extension needed, browser-based
  • Pricing: Free tier with 3 hours per session and 10-minute wait between sessions; Premium $5/month for unlimited

Best For: Users who need to watch content from websites that block embedding or aren't supported elsewhere.

8. Metastream

A free, open-source browser extension that adds synchronized playback to various sites.

  • Supports: YouTube, Twitch, Netflix, Hulu, Vimeo, Dailymotion, SoundCloud
  • Features: Built-in chat, queue system, unlimited users
  • Pricing: Free and open source
  • Note: Chrome/Firefox extension required

Best For: Users who prefer open-source software and don't mind installing an extension.

Choosing the Right Platform

Different apps excel in different areas. Here's a quick guide based on your needs:

  • For streaming services (Netflix, Disney+): Teleparty has the widest service support; Scener offers video chat
  • For YouTube/Twitch: Watch4Gether, Watch2Gether, OpenTogetherTube, and Metastream all work well
  • For mobile-first users: Rave has native apps; Watch4Gether and Watch2Gether work in mobile browsers
  • For video/voice chat built-in: Scener or Rave
  • For public room discovery: Watch2Gether. Many users also pair watch parties with Discord for voice chat
  • For open-source preference: OpenTogetherTube or Metastream
  • For any website: Hyperbeam's virtual browser approach
  • For no signup/extension: Watch4Gether, Watch2Gether, OpenTogetherTube, or Hyperbeam
